My work takes a broad, multi-tool approach to global hydrology. I do this across millions of rivers by leveraging and developing tools from hydrology, biogeochemistry, remote sensing, and large-scale geoprocessing. I’m particularly interested in what arises from simultaneously considering hydrology and biogeochemistry at large scales: how might a broad, ‘bio-geo-morpho-chemical’ approach reveal new knowledge about global surface water quantity and quality?

Measuring rivers from space

  • What properties of global rivers can we measure using satellite measurements?
  • What do these measurements reveal about global hydrology?
  • Representative papers:
    • Constraining remote river discharge estimation using reach-scale geomorphology
    • Inversion of river discharge from remotely sensed river widths: A critical assessment at three-thousand global river gauges
    • Remotely sensing river greenhouse gas exchange velocity using the SWOT satellite

Transporting ‘stuff’ downstream

  • How do organic matter, pollutants, and other solutes move through drainage systems?
  • How does geomorphology and hydrology modulate downstream water quality?
  • Representative papers:
    • Lake morphometry and river network controls on evasion of terrestrially sourced headwater CO2
    • Watershed DOC uptake occurs mostly in lakes in the summer and in rivers in the winter
    • Global controls on DOC reaction versus export in watersheds: A Damkhler number analysis
